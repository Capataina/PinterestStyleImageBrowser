# Algorithmic Effects on the Diversity of Consumption on Spotify

## Source reference

- ACM Web Conf 2020: https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3366423.3380281
- Franklin & Zhang follow-up: https://beckyxzhang.github.io/paper

## Claim summary

Empirical study of Spotify's recommendation diversity. Finds that **high consumption diversity is strongly associated with long-term user metrics** (conversion, retention). Diversity is not just an ethical / fairness concern — it's a business-quality KPI.

## Relevance to our project

A4: Substantive backing for the "diversity-aware retrieval" design direction. The project's 7-tier sampler is the right product instinct; this paper provides the *citable evidence* that diversification increases value.

A1: For the diversity-aware retrieval recommendation downstream, this is one of the authoritative sources backing the case.

## Specific takeaways

- The ICML paper "Assessing the diversity-accuracy dilemma" (Franklin & Zhang) explicitly studies how diversity vs accuracy interact — useful framing for the recommendation downstream.
- Spotify's research validates "users prefer recommendations they didn't expect over the absolute top-rated" — same thesis as the project's tiered sampler.
